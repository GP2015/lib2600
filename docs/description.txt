This document serves as a comprehensive description of the NTSC Atari 2600's functionality (as well as all the relevant peripheral devices' logic functionalities) and has been used as a reference for the development of the lib2600 emulator. Due to its comprehensive nature, this document is extremely verbose and technical.

1. Console

1.1 Pinout

The base console has a 24-pin cartridge port, two 9-pin controller ports, three external logic switches, two buttons, and an RF signal output. It also features a power input port and an external power switch, however these will be ignored as they don't affect the logic circuitry in a meaningful way.

The three external logic switches are as follows:
- TV Type (LOW for black & white output, HIGH for colour output)
- Left Difficulty (LOW for B, HIGH for A)
- Right Difficulty (LOW for B, HIGH for A)

The two external logic buttons are as follows:
- Game Select (LOW when pressed, HIGH otherwise)
- Game Reset (LOW when pressed, HIGH otherwise)

The 24 pins in the cartridge port are used as follows;
- 13 pins for the 13-bit address bus (intended to be output-only, but of course the cartridge can write to it anyway, resulting in bus contention)
- 8 pins for the 8-bit data bus (bi-directional)
- 1 pin for power output to the cartridge (ignored here)
- 1 pin for digital ground (ignored here)
- 1 pin for shield ground (ignored here)

The 9 pins in either of the controller ports are used as follows:
- 7 pins for transmitting data
- 1 pin for power output to the peripheral (ignored here)
- 1 pin for video ground (ignored here)

1.2 Chips

1.2.1 Overview

The easiest way of describing the black box behaviour of the console is by describing the black box behaviour of the console's internal chips and the interactions between them.

The console contains three different chips:
- the 1.19 MHz MOS Technology 6507 microcontroller, acting as the CPU of the system
- the MOS Technology 6532 RAM-I/O-Timer (RIOT), acting as the RAM, timer, and I/O handler of the system
- the Television Interface Adapter (TIA), a custom chip acting as the audio & video processor of the system
